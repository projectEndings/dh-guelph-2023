What do we mean by dependency?

  • Something outside of your control but on which you depend
  • Some dependencies in life are unavoidable (air, for instance)
  • But others are
  • And we employ mitigation strategies all the time: charging your laptop in case you forget your charger

External Dependencies

  • Resources that promise to stay around and/or continue working
  • Digital dependencies are liable to disappear, break down, change their terms, et cetera

How to Determine Dependability (aka Digital Peer Review)

  • Who wrote it? Do you trust the developer, institution, organiation?
  • Does it have any dependencies?
  • How frequently updated? How responsive are the developers?
  • When was the last release?
  • How well is it documented?

Dependencies Beyond Code

  • Note that dependencies are not just technical -- they are also social, infrastructural, institutional
  • You lose access to a server; your computer dies; the archive loses funding

Enumerate Your Dependencies

  • On whom do you depend? Archivists, developers, contributors, PIs?
  • On what do you depend? A web server, a mapping library, an image slider?
